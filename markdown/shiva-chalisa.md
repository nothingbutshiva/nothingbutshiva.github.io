# शिव चालीसा — Shiva Chalisa

The Shiva Chalisa is a traditional devotional hymn of forty verses (*chaupai*) offered in praise of Lord Shiva — the auspicious one, the destroyer and transformer within the Hindu trinity. Recited by devotees seeking blessings, protection, and spiritual liberation, this chalisa celebrates Shiva's many forms, deeds, and attributes. Below is the Hindi text in Devanagari script followed by an original English translation for each section.

---

## Doha (दोहा) — Opening Couplets

### Doha

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

जय गणेश गिरिजा सुवन, मंगल मूल सुजान।
कहत अयोध्यादास तुम, देहु अभय वरदान॥

**English Translation:**

Glory to Ganesha, the son of Girija, the wise root of all auspiciousness.
Ayodhyadas humbly implores: grant me the boon of fearlessness.

---

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

जय गिरिजा पति दीनदयाला, सदा करत संतन प्रतिपाला।
भाल चन्द्रमा सोहत नीके, कानन कुण्डल नागफनी के॥

**English Translation:**

Glory to the lord of Girija, compassionate to the humble, ever the protector of the devout.
The crescent moon adorns his brow beautifully; serpent-hood earrings grace his ears.

---

## Chaupai (चौपाई) — The Forty Verses

### Chaupai 1

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

अंग गौर शिर गंग बहाये, मुण्डमाल तन क्षार लगाये।
वस्त्र खाल बाघम्बर सोहे, छवि को देख नाग मुनि मोहे॥

**English Translation:**

His body fair, the Ganga flowing from his head, wearing a garland of skulls, ash smeared upon his form.
Clad in a tiger skin that shines with splendor — beholding his beauty, even serpent-gods and sages are enchanted.

---

### Chaupai 2

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

मैना मातु की हवै दुलारी, बाम अंग सोहत छवि न्यारी।
कर त्रिशूल सोहत छवि भारी, करत सदा शत्रुन क्षयकारी॥

**English Translation:**

The beloved daughter of mother Maina sits at his left side, her presence a vision of unique grace.
The trident in his hand radiates a mighty splendor, ever bringing destruction upon all enemies.

---

### Chaupai 3

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

नन्दी गणेश सोहैं तहँ कैसे, सागर मध्य कमल हैं जैसे।
कार्तिक श्याम और गणराऊ, या छवि को कहि जात न काऊ॥

**English Translation:**

Nandi and Ganesha attend him there, like lotuses blooming in the midst of the ocean.
With Kartikeya, the dark-hued one, and the lord of the Ganas — who could ever describe this vision?

---

### Chaupai 4

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

देवन जबहीं जाय पुकारा, तबहीं दुख प्रभु आप निवारा।
किया उपद्रव तारक भारी, देवन सब मिलि तुमहिं जुहारी॥

**English Translation:**

Whenever the gods come crying out in distress, at once the Lord himself removes their suffering.
When the demon Taraka wreaked great havoc, all the gods united came to bow before you.

---

### Chaupai 5

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

तुरत षडानन आप पठायउ, लवनिमेष महँ मारि गिरायउ।
आप जलन्धर असुर संहारा, सुयश तुम्हार विदित संसारा॥

**English Translation:**

At once you sent the six-faced one (Kartikeya), who slew the demon in the blink of an eye.
You yourself destroyed the demon Jalandhara — your glorious fame is known throughout the world.

---

### Chaupai 6

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

त्रिपुरासुर सन युद्ध मचाई, सबहिं कृपा कर लीन बचाई।
किया तपहिं भागीरथ भारी, पुरब प्रतिज्ञा तासु पुरारी॥

**English Translation:**

You waged war against Tripurasura, and by your grace saved everyone.
Bhagiratha performed great austerities, and you, O destroyer of cities, fulfilled his ancient vow.

---

### Chaupai 7

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

दानिन महँ तुम समतुल्य कहाँ, अद्भुत लीला तुम्हारी जहाँ।
करि कृपा जन की जो सुनहू, ताको दुख निकट नहिं अवहू॥

**English Translation:**

Among the generous, who could ever equal you? Wondrous is your divine play wherever it unfolds.
Those whose prayers you hear with grace — sorrow never comes near them.

---

### Chaupai 8

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

तुम्हरे दरस परस सुख होई, तुम्हरे पूजे सब सुख पाई।
तुम सबके हृदयन में बसहू, विषम शूल कालहिं तुम नसहू॥

**English Translation:**

Joy arises from beholding and touching your form; by worshipping you, all happiness is attained.
You dwell within the hearts of all, and you destroy the dreadful pain of death itself.

---

### Chaupai 9

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

तुम्हरो नाम जपत सुख होई, अघ विदारन सुजस तिहारो।
सब पातक नासहिं नर तेरो, जे तव चरन सरन निज हेरो॥

**English Translation:**

Chanting your name brings bliss; your fair renown is the destroyer of all sin.
All transgressions are annihilated for those who take refuge at your feet as their own.

---

### Chaupai 10

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

केहि विधि स्तुति करौं महादेवा, कहत सिद्ध मुनि असुर सुर सेवा।
शिव सिव सिव सदा उचरहीं, सकल मनोरथ पूरन करहीं॥

**English Translation:**

In what manner shall I praise you, O great Lord? Siddhas, sages, demons, and gods all serve you.
Those who ceaselessly chant "Shiva, Shiva, Shiva" — all their heart's desires are fulfilled.

---

### Chaupai 11

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

कामी क्रोधी लोभी पापी, जे तव सरन पड़े प्रभु आपी।
ते सब उद्धरि गए संसारा, एहि विधि सब जग जान तिहारा॥

**English Translation:**

The lustful, the wrathful, the greedy, the sinful — those who fall at your refuge, O Lord,
they are all delivered across this world. In this way, the whole world knows your greatness.

---

### Chaupai 12

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

धूप दीप नैवेद्य चढ़ावें, शंकर सम्मुख पाठ सुनावें।
जन्म जन्म के पाप नसावें, अन्त धाम शिवपुर में पावें॥

**English Translation:**

Those who offer incense, lamp, and food offerings, and recite this prayer before Shankara —
the sins of countless lifetimes are destroyed, and in the end they attain the abode of Shiva.

---

### Chaupai 13

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

तुम अतर्क्य अगोचर देवा, तुम्हरे भजन करें सब मेवा।
नित नेमी ज्ञानी गुण गावें, रोगन ते सदा बचि जावें॥

**English Translation:**

You are beyond reasoning and beyond the senses, O Lord; all fruits come from devotion to you.
Those disciplined souls and the wise who sing your virtues are ever protected from all diseases.

---

### Chaupai 14

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

चन्द्रमुखी पद्मासन ध्यावें, शंकर जी को नित पूजावें।
काशी में प्रभु मृत्यु जो पावें, शिवपुर ताको आवागमन मिटावें॥

**English Translation:**

Those who meditate upon the moon-faced Lord seated in the lotus posture, who daily worship Shankara —
those who meet death at Kashi in the Lord's presence, for them Shivapura ends the cycle of rebirth.

---

### Chaupai 15

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

अपने दासन को तुम तारो, जन की विपति हरो त्रिपुरारी।
सूखे को सागर जल दीजे, दीन दयालु करुणा कीजे॥

**English Translation:**

O you who ferry your servants across, remover of your devotees' calamities, O destroyer of Tripura!
Give the ocean's water to the parched; O compassionate one, bestow your mercy.

---

### Chaupai 16

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

गुणगावत मुनि चारिउ वेदा, शिव की महिमा कही न जाये।
शिव प्रभु की कला अपारा, केहि विधि करें तिहार विचारा॥

**English Translation:**

The sages sing your virtues, so do the four Vedas — yet the glory of Shiva cannot be told.
Boundless are the arts and powers of Lord Shiva; by what means could one fully contemplate you?

---

### Chaupai 17

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

शिव शम्भु की महिमा भारी, रवि शशि पायन आरत हारी।
ग्रह नक्षत्र सब आज्ञाकारी, विश्वम्भर की कृपा तिहारी॥

**English Translation:**

Immense is the glory of Shiva Shambhu, who removes the distress of the sun and moon at his feet.
All planets and stars obey his command — the grace of the sustainer of the universe is yours.

---

### Chaupai 18

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

कामदेव को तुम जारे, ताके बाद पुनह जिवारे।
सती जो रूठी तुमसे भारी, ताहू को सत सम्मति सारी॥

**English Translation:**

You burned Kamadeva (the god of desire) to ashes, and afterwards restored him to life again.
When Sati was deeply angered with you, even her you guided back to righteous understanding.

---

### Chaupai 19

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

नन्दी भृंगी बिन रहि न जाये, सदा ध्यान में तुम्हें लगाये।
गण किन्नर हैं तुम्हरे सेवा, करत सदा रहते शिव देवा॥

**English Translation:**

Nandi and Bhringi cannot remain without you, their minds ever absorbed in meditation upon you.
Ganas and Kinnaras are in your service, always attending to Lord Shiva.

---

### Chaupai 20

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

कहते अयोध्यादास तिहारी, सुनि शम्भो कर कृपा हमारी।
रीझि है तू देवन हितकारी, जन सुख दायक सब दुख हारी॥

**English Translation:**

Ayodhyadas proclaims your praise — hear him, O Shambhu, and bestow grace upon us.
Be pleased, O benefactor of the gods, giver of joy to devotees and remover of all sorrows.

---

### Chaupai 21

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

शम्भु चरन में जो शिर नावें, विनय करें सो सुख फल पावें।
कोटि कोटि ब्रह्माण्ड बनावें, कोटि काल प्रलयन मिटावें॥

**English Translation:**

Those who bow their heads at Shambhu's feet, who pray with humility, receive the fruit of happiness.
He creates millions upon millions of universes and in countless ages dissolves them all.

---

### Chaupai 22

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

विषय वासना सब नसि जाई, जो शिव चालीसा निज गाई।
निर्भय ताहि कदे भय नाहीं, सब सुख सम्पति पावहि ताहीं॥

**English Translation:**

All worldly desires and attachments are destroyed for the one who sings this Shiva Chalisa.
Fearless they become — never again shall fear touch them — and they attain all joy and prosperity.

---

### Chaupai 23

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

गौरा ध्यान धरे जो मन लाई, ताको साधन सिद्धि सुहाई।
संतति सम्पति सुख सब पावें, शिव चरनन में मन अति लावें॥

**English Translation:**

One who meditates on Gaura (Parvati) with a focused mind achieves beautiful success in spiritual practice.
They attain children, wealth, and every happiness — their mind deeply devoted to Shiva's feet.

---

### Chaupai 24

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

यह शिव चालीसा जो गावे, जन मन वांछित फल सो पावे।
नर हो या नारी शिव ध्यावे, सुखद मुक्ति शम्भु तब पावे॥

**English Translation:**

Whoever sings this Shiva Chalisa obtains the fruit their heart desires.
Whether man or woman, those who meditate on Shiva then receive blissful liberation from Shambhu.

---

### Chaupai 25

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

प्रेम सहित जो शिव गुण गावें, मन वांछित फल शीघ्र ही पावें।
बारहमास सितउ दिन रातू, शम्भु ध्यान करो मन के साथू॥

**English Translation:**

Those who sing Shiva's virtues with love swiftly receive the fruit their heart desires.
Throughout all twelve months, day and night through the cold, meditate on Shambhu with all your heart.

---

### Chaupai 26

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

ब्रह्मा विष्णु सदा शिव ध्यावें, भोलेनाथ के नाम सुनावें।
वेद पुराण की वाणी बोलें, शंकर की महिमा मन डोलें॥

**English Translation:**

Brahma and Vishnu ever meditate upon Shiva, proclaiming the name of Bholenath.
They speak the words of the Vedas and Puranas, their hearts swaying with the glory of Shankara.

---

### Chaupai 27

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

तीनों लोक से न्यारे शम्भू, ब्रह्मा विष्णु महेश स्वयम्भू।
सकल सृष्टि तुम्हीं ने बनाई, रचना तुम्हरी कही न जाई॥

**English Translation:**

Shambhu stands apart from all three worlds; Brahma, Vishnu, and Mahesha — he is the self-existent.
You alone have created the entire creation; your handiwork is beyond description.

---

### Chaupai 28

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

जटा में गंगा धार बहाये, त्रिपुण्ड भाल पे शोभित साजे।
हर हर शम्भू सब जन बोलें, अमृत वाणी मुख से बोलें॥

**English Translation:**

A stream of Ganga flows through his matted locks; the tripundra mark adorns his brow in splendor.
All people chant "Hara, Hara, Shambhu!" — words of nectar flowing from their lips.

---

### Chaupai 29

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

विषपान किया जग हितकारी, नीलकण्ठ नाम तब धारी।
समुद्र मन्थन में अमृत पाये, सुरन को अमृत तुम पिलाये॥

**English Translation:**

You drank poison for the welfare of the world, and then bore the name Neelakantha (the blue-throated one).
When nectar arose from the churning of the ocean, you gave it to the gods to drink.

---

### Chaupai 30

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

अर्धनारीश्वर रूप निरालाँ, जिसमें शक्ति और शिव का मेला।
रौद्र रूप में जग भय खाये, ताण्डव नृत्य सदा मन भाये॥

**English Translation:**

Unique is the form of Ardhanarishvara, in which Shakti and Shiva are united as one.
In your fierce form the world trembles with fear, yet your Tandava dance ever captivates the mind.

---

### Chaupai 31

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

डमरू बजाये नाद सुनाये, शिव तांडव से सृष्टि हिलाये।
गिरिजा संग विहार करें जब, सुख शान्ति बरसे सब तब॥

**English Translation:**

You sound the damaru drum and make the cosmic vibration heard; with Shiva's Tandava the creation trembles.
When you wander joyfully with Girija, then bliss and peace rain down upon all.

---

### Chaupai 32

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

सोमवार को तुम्हें पुजारें, जल अभिषेक शिवलिंग सँवारें।
बेलपत्र फूल चन्दन सोहे, भक्त तुम्हारा दर्शन चोहे॥

**English Translation:**

On Monday your devotees worship you, bathing the Shivalinga with water and adorning it.
Bel leaves, flowers, and sandalwood paste shine there — your devotees yearn for a glimpse of you.

---

### Chaupai 33

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

बारह ज्योतिर्लिंग जग माहीं, जहाँ जहाँ प्रभु तुम विराजाहीं।
काशी काँची अवन्तिका सोहे, महाकाल दरबार न मोहे॥

**English Translation:**

Twelve Jyotirlingas shine throughout the world, wherever you, O Lord, are seated in splendor.
Kashi, Kanchi, and Avantika gleam — who is not enchanted by the court of Mahakala?

---

### Chaupai 34

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

शिवरात्रि को जागे जो कोई, अन्न दान पुण्य फल होई।
रुद्राभिषेक जो नर करि जाई, जनम जनम की बाधा मिटि जाई॥

**English Translation:**

Whoever keeps vigil on Shivaratri, they earn the merit of giving food in charity.
The one who performs the Rudrabhisheka — the obstacles of birth after birth are erased.

---

### Chaupai 35

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

पञ्चाक्षर मन्त्र जो जापें, शिव कृपा तब शीघ्र ही व्यापें।
ओम नमः शिवाय उचारें, भव बंधन के बन्ध निवारें॥

**English Translation:**

Those who chant the five-syllable mantra — Shiva's grace swiftly pervades them.
Uttering "Om Namah Shivaya," they break free from the bonds of worldly existence.

---

### Chaupai 36

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

सन्तन के तुम सदा सहाई, भक्ति मार्ग में राह दिखाई।
दीन दयालु कृपा निधि शम्भू, ब्रह्मा विष्णु सदा बन्दे शम्भू॥

**English Translation:**

You are ever the helper of the saintly, showing the way on the path of devotion.
Compassionate to the lowly, a treasury of mercy is Shambhu — Brahma and Vishnu forever bow to Shambhu.

---

### Chaupai 37

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

शरणागत की रक्षा करिये, सकल विपत्ति को दूर हरिये।
मन से ध्यान शम्भु का लावें, सकल मनोरथ पूरन पावें॥

**English Translation:**

Protect those who come to your refuge; sweep away all their calamities.
Those who fix their mind in meditation upon Shambhu attain the fulfillment of all their wishes.

---

### Chaupai 38

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

जो यह पाठ करे मन लाई, ताकी शम्भू सुनें सुनाई।
मन क्रम वचन ध्यान जो धरही, उसके ताप शम्भू सब हरही॥

**English Translation:**

Whoever recites this prayer with a focused mind — Shambhu hears their call.
Those who hold him in thought, deed, and word — Shambhu removes all their afflictions.

---

### Chaupai 39

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

राम राम रट सदा सुहाई, शम्भु शम्भु मुख मंगल गाई।
सकल कामना हृदय की पूरें, दूर करे सब दुख अति दूरें॥

**English Translation:**

The chanting of "Rama, Rama" is ever delightful; singing "Shambhu, Shambhu" brings auspiciousness from the lips.
All the desires of the heart are fulfilled, and every sorrow is driven far, far away.

---

### Chaupai 40

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

शिव चालीसा जो नित गावे, सब सुख भोग मुक्ति फल पावे।
अयोध्यादास कहत शम्भू की, जय जय जय शिव शम्भू की॥

**English Translation:**

Whoever daily sings the Shiva Chalisa attains all worldly joy and the fruit of liberation.
Ayodhyadas proclaims the glory of Shambhu — victory, victory, victory to Shiva Shambhu!

---

## Doha (दोहा) — Closing Couplet

### Doha

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

शिव चालीसा पाठ कर, शम्भू पद नित ध्याय।
अयोध्यादास प्रभू कहत, सदा अनन्द मनाय॥

**English Translation:**

Having recited the Shiva Chalisa, meditating daily at Shambhu's feet —
Ayodhyadas says to the Lord: may eternal bliss be celebrated always.

---
