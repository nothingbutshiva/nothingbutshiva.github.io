# Shiva Aarti — Om Jai Shiv Omkara (ॐ जय शिव ओमकारा)

Aarti is a devotional ritual of light performed across Hindu traditions, in which a lamp — usually fueled by ghee or oil — is circled before a deity while hymns of praise are sung. "Om Jai Shiv Omkara" is among the most widely recited aartis dedicated to Lord Shiva. Sung in homes and temples throughout India, it celebrates Shiva's many divine attributes — his cosmic form, his compassion, and his power to liberate devotees from suffering. The hymn is in the public domain and has been passed down through centuries of oral and written tradition.

Below are all the verses of this traditional aarti in Devanagari Hindi, each followed by an original English translation.

---

### Verse 1

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

ॐ जय शिव ओमकारा,
स्वामी जय शिव ओमकारा।  
ब्रह्मा, विष्णु, सदाशिव,
अर्धांगी धारा॥
ॐ जय शिव ओमकारा॥

**English Translation:**

Om — glory to you, Shiva, the sacred syllable made manifest,
O Lord, glory to you, embodiment of Om.
Brahma the creator, Vishnu the sustainer, and eternal Shiva —
you bear the divine feminine as half your very form.
Om, glory to Shiva Omkara.

---

### Verse 2

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

एकानन चतुरानन पंचानन राजे,
हंसासन गरुड़ासन वृषवाहन साजे।  
ॐ जय शिव ओमकारा॥

**English Translation:**

With one face, with four faces, with five faces you shine in splendour —
seated upon the swan, upon the eagle, adorned with the bull as your mount.
Om, glory to Shiva Omkara.

---

### Verse 3

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

दो भुज चार चतुर्भुज दसभुज अति सोहे,
त्रिगुण रूप निरखता त्रिभुवन जन मोहे।  
ॐ जय शिव ओमकारा॥

**English Translation:**

With two arms, with four arms, with ten arms you are resplendent —
beholding your form of the three qualities, the people of all three worlds are enchanted.
Om, glory to Shiva Omkara.

---

### Verse 4

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

अक्षमाला वनमाला मुण्डमाला धारी,
त्रिपुरारी कंसारी कर माला धारी।  
ॐ जय शिव ओमकारा॥

**English Translation:**

You wear a rosary of sacred beads, a garland of forest flowers, and a garland of skulls —
destroyer of the three cities, slayer of Kansa, you hold the prayer beads in your hand.
Om, glory to Shiva Omkara.

---

### Verse 5

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

श्वेताम्बर पीताम्बर बाघम्बर अंगे,
सनकादिक गरुणादिक भूतादिक संगे।  
ॐ जय शिव ओमकारा॥

**English Translation:**

Clad in white robes, in yellow silks, and in a tiger skin upon your body —
the sages like Sanaka, the celestial beings like Garuda, and the spirits all attend you.
Om, glory to Shiva Omkara.

---

### Verse 6

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

कर के मध्य कमण्डलु चक्र त्रिशूलधारी,
जगकर्ता जगभर्ता जगसंहारी।  
ॐ जय शिव ओमकारा॥

**English Translation:**

In your hands you hold the water vessel, the discus, and the trident —
you are the creator of the world, the sustainer of the world, and the destroyer of the world.
Om, glory to Shiva Omkara.

---

### Verse 7

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

ब्रह्मा विष्णु सदाशिव जानत अविवेका,
प्रणवाक्षर में शोभित ये तीनों एका।  
ॐ जय शिव ओमकारा॥

**English Translation:**

Brahma, Vishnu, and eternal Shiva — the unwise regard them as separate,
yet within the sacred syllable Om, these three shine as one.
Om, glory to Shiva Omkara.

---

### Verse 8

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

लक्ष्मी व सावित्री पार्वती संगा,
पार्वती अर्धांगी, शिवलहरी गंगा।  
ॐ जय शिव ओमकारा॥

**English Translation:**

Lakshmi and Saraswati accompany their lords, and Parvati stands with Shiva —
Parvati is his other half, and the Ganga flows in waves from his crown.
Om, glory to Shiva Omkara.

---

### Verse 9

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

तत्पुरुष नाम सदाशिव अघोरी अकारा,
ओंकार प्रणवाक्षर ये पांच विचारा।  
ॐ जय शिव ओमकारा॥

**English Translation:**

Tatpurusha, Sadashiva, and Aghora — these are among his names and forms;
together with Omkara and the syllable of creation, contemplate these five aspects.
Om, glory to Shiva Omkara.

---

### Verse 10

**Hindi (हिन्दी):**

निर्गुण शिवजी की आरती जो कोई नर गावे,
कहत शिवानन्द स्वामी मनवांछित फल पावे।  
ॐ जय शिव ओमकारा॥

**English Translation:**

Whoever sings this aarti of the formless Lord Shiva —
so declares Swami Shivanand — shall receive the fruit of every heart's desire.
Om, glory to Shiva Omkara.

---

*ॐ नमः शिवाय*
*Om Namah Shivaya*
